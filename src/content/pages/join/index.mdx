<section id="network">
  <h2>2) Connect to the network</h2>
  <p>Austin Mesh currently supports 2 networking technologies. <strong>We recommend MeshCore for all beginners</strong>.</p>
  <ol>
    <li>MeshCore: Best for text message communication</li>
    <li>Meshtastic: Best for data(e.g. weather, air quality) and travel</li>
  </ol>
  <p>(<small><a href="/about/meshcore-vs-meshtastic/">In depth comparison of MeshCore and Meshtastic</a></small>)</p>

  <h3>Getting started using MeshCore</h3>
  <details>
    <summary>1) Hardware/Radio</summary>
    <p>You'll need a radio to get started. Prices range from $13 - hundreds. Many sit in the $30-80 range.</p>
    <p>Find our <a href="/devices/#options">suggested hardware on the devices page</a>.</p>
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>2) Software Setup</summary>
    <ol>
      <li>Flash the MeshCore firmware to your radio using <a href="https://flasher.meshcore.io/" rel="nofollow noopener">flasher.meshcore.io</a>.</li>
      <li>Download the MeshCore app to your phone.</li>
      <li>Connect to your radio via bluetooth.</li>
      <li>Select the <strong>USA/Canada (Recommended)</strong> preset.</li>
      <li><strong>Do not adjust</strong> frequency, bandwidth, spreading factor, or coding rate.</li>
      <li>Set your name. This will be visible to others on the network.</li>
      <li><strong>optional but recommended:</strong> turn on 2-byte path size under <strong>Experimental Settings</strong>. (<a href="/join/getting-started-meshcore/#2-byte-paths">Learn more about Meshcore 2-byte routing</a>)</li>
      <li>Having trouble? View our full <a href="/join/getting-started-meshcore/">Getting Started with MeshCore guide</a> or <a href="https://discord.gg/6a5Sv2s9bG">join our Discord server</a>.</li>
    </ol>
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>3) Channels and Use</summary>
    <ol>
      <li>Austin Mesh uses the default <strong>Public</strong> channel that is automatically configured.</li>
      <li>Optionally, you may add #testing and #meetup.</li>
      <li>Send your first message in <strong>Public</strong>. Does it say "Heard &#123;x&#125; repeat(s)"? If so, that means you're connected!<ul><li>If not, try moving your radio. The higher/fewer obstructions the better.</li></ul></li>
      <li><strong>Send away</strong>! It's very unlikely you'll bother anyone by being chatty or saying hello a bunch.</li>
      <li>Consider <a href="https://discord.gg/6a5Sv2s9bG">joining the Discord server</a> for more advice.</li>
    </ol>
  </details>
  <h3>Getting started using Meshtastic</h3>
  <details>
    <summary>1) Hardware/Radio</summary>
    <p>You'll need a radio to get started. Prices range from $13 - hundreds. Many sit in the $30-80 range.</p>
    <p>Find our <a href="/devices/#options">suggested hardware on the devices page</a>.</p>
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>2) Software Setup</summary>
    <ol>
      <li>Flash the Meshtastic firmware to your radio using <a href="https://flasher.meshtastic.org/" rel="nofollow noopener">flasher.meshtastic.org</a>.</li>
      <li>Download the Meshtastic app to your phone.</li>
      <li>Connect to your radio via bluetooth.</li>
      <li>Select the <strong>United States</strong> region.</li>
      <li>Select the <strong>Long Range - Fast</strong> preset.</li>
      <li><strong>Turn on</strong>: Ignore MQTT, OK to MQTT</li>
      <li><strong>Do not adjust</strong> frequency slot, frequency override.</li>
      <li>Set your Long Name and Short Name. These will be visible to others on the network.</li>
    </ol>
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>3) Channels and Use</summary>
    <ol>
      <li>Austin Mesh uses the default <strong>Primary Channel</strong> that is automatically configured.</li>
      <li>Send your first message in <strong>Public</strong>. Does it say "Acknowledged"? If so, that means you're connected!<ul><li>If it says "Max Retransmission Reached" try moving your radio. The higher/fewer obstructions the better.</li></ul></li>
      <li><strong>Send away</strong>! It's very unlikely you'll bother anyone by being chatty or saying hello a bunch.</li>
      <li>Consider <a href="https://discord.gg/6a5Sv2s9bG">joining the Discord server</a> for more advice.</li>
    </ol>
  </details>
</section>
