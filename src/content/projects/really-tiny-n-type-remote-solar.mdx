# Really Tiny N-Type with Remote Solar

<Image src={nTypeConnector} alt="The completed tiny junction-box node with its N-Type antenna connector" widths={[480, 768, 1024]} sizes="(min-width: 720px) 700px, 100vw" />

This was a build for a unique situation - the node itself "needed" to use an Alfa antenna, be extremely discreet, and be mounted somewhere the sun didn't reach.

## Parts List

- [2.5"x2.2"x1.4" junction box](https://amzn.to/4eSmv79)
- [1w USB C Solar Panel](https://amzn.to/4ugvunx)
- [180° USB C Female to Male Adapter](https://amzn.to/4ujuq2j)
- [RAK 19003](https://www.rfindex.com/mesh/devices/rak-rak19003-4631)
- [Ipex/U.fl to N-Type Bulkhead](https://amzn.to/4fov7T8)
- [Drain Plug](https://amzn.to/3FQVZf5)
- [PG9 Cable Gland/Passthrough](https://amzn.to/3R5zskj)
- [800mah 503048 LiPo Battery](https://amzn.to/3RJLWOA)

<Image src={internals} alt="Inside view of the junction box showing the RAK 19003, battery, and short interconnects packed into the tiny enclosure" widths={[480, 768, 1024]} sizes="(min-width: 720px) 700px, 100vw" />

## Results

It performed fine, although the Alfa continually disappoints in comparison to other antennas. It was installed for 9 months and then the location was no longer needed.

## What I'd do different

<Image src={solarVent} alt="External view showing the USB-C solar panel passthrough and the drain plug vent on the side of the junction box" widths={[480, 768, 1024]} sizes="(min-width: 720px) 700px, 100vw" />

Technically I used a PG7 gland which was too tight to easily pass USB-C through, which is why I recommended PG9. Other than that, this build was successful for the use case. If at all possible, I would go with a better antenna, though!
