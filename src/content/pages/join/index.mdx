<hgroup>
  # Choosing a platform

  Austin Mesh currently supports 2 networking technologies. **We recommend MeshCore for all beginners**. If you want the nitty-gritty, check out the [in depth comparison of MeshCore and Meshtastic](/about/meshcore-vs-meshtastic/).
</hgroup>

## MeshCore (Recommended)

If you want to be able to send text messages to your friends, family, or the broader Austin Mesh community, you want to use MeshCore. The technology is decentralized and encrypted, allowing you resilient and secure messaging. As of June 2026, it also provides very _reliable_ messaging in most of the greater Austin area.

<a href="/join/meshcore-setup/" type="button">MeshCore Setup Guide</a>

### How it works

MeshCore relies on 2 types of radios(nodes): Companions and Repeaters.

- **Companions** are the radios you send messages from and generally carry on your person. They receive and send messages, but **do not** retransmit messages from _other_ companions.

- **Repeaters** are typically fixed location radios powered by a solar panel and battery. Repeaters are what help the network grow and become more resilient.

### Features

MeshCore supports private(direct) messages, public group chats(channels), private channels, mapping, trace routes, signal coverage estimation, and many other quality of life features.

## Meshtastic

If you want to collect telemetry data such as weather stations, motion sensors, GPS position logs, or other sensor data, Meshtastic is the technology for you. The Meshtastic network in Austin covers a similar geographic area, but due to technology constraints is far less reliable for text messaging than MeshCore.

<a href="/join/meshtastic-setup/" type="button">Meshtastic Setup Guide</a>

### How it works

Meshtastic uses 12 different types of radios depending on your needs. The full details are available on the [Meshtastic Docs](https://meshtastic.org/docs/configuration/radio/device/) but we generally recommend every radio start with **Client** and **Client Base**, only switching once a need is demonstrated.

### Features

Meshtastic supports private(direct) messages, public group chats(channels), private channels, mapping, trace routes, sensor integration, ATAK integration, and more.
